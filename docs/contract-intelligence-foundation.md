# Contract Intelligence Foundation

Contract Intelligence organizes immutable contract-document revisions, relationships, incorporation edges, exact clauses, defined terms, normalized requirements, deterministic deadline candidates, internal notice drafts, obligation views, correspondence links, cost/delay/condition evidence, chronologies, conflicts, dashboards, and cited Q&A.

Exact contractual language and citations remain separate from non-controlling summaries. Conflicting definitions, clauses, recipients, dates, and requirements are preserved for qualified human review. Contract requirements and obligations reference canonical workflow records through stable IDs; they do not replace project changes, RFIs, submittals, procurement, schedule, daily reports, risks, or commissioning records.

Deadline calculations support calendar, business, and working days, including configured holidays. Calculations are withheld when trigger dates, periods, or calendar bases are ambiguous. Candidate deadlines are explainable proposals, not legal advice.

Notice candidates connect project evidence to potentially relevant clauses. Drafts remain internal, versioned, cited, and `external_delivery_performed=false`. Notifications use Brunel's local-only outbox. No email or correspondence adapter sends externally.

Brunel does not provide legal advice or determine entitlement, breach, liability, responsibility, compensability, excusability, concurrency, critical-path delay, damages, waiver, enforceability, notice sufficiency, or litigation strategy. It does not issue notices, send correspondence, approve changes, commit money, modify contracts, or sign documents.
