# Commissioning & Turnover Intelligence

This module organizes project-scoped commissioning systems, assets, cited requirements, checklist revisions, immutable test-procedure revisions, reported test executions, instrument evidence, deficiencies, readiness assessments, and turnover packages. Canonical document revisions and workflow records are referenced by stable IDs and citations—including optional visual regions—rather than copied or mutated.

Product lineage keeps specified, submitted, procured, delivered, installed, tested, and turned-over identities distinct. Conflicts become review findings. Expected results, reported results, and reviewer-confirmed results are separate fields. Retests link to prior executions without deleting failed history. Deficiency verification and closure require authorized human rationale and evidence.

Readiness is deterministic and explainable: `insufficient_evidence`, `not_ready`, `readiness_concerns`, or `ready_for_human_review`. Only an authorized user may record confirmed readiness. Turnover completeness is a proposal; package acceptance remains a recorded human disposition. Notifications remain in the local outbox.

The API, CLI, dashboards, search, and cited Q&A are project-scoped. Extension points use workflow links for drawings, submittals, procurement, RFIs, changes, meetings, daily reports, schedule, and risk records.

Brunel never certifies systems, approves tests, accepts equipment, authorizes startup or energization, waives deficiencies, confirms substantial/final/contractual completion, determines engineering/code/safety compliance, authorizes occupancy, releases retainage, or sends external communications.
