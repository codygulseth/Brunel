# Enterprise Project Intelligence

Enterprise Intelligence creates explicitly authorized organization portfolios without weakening Brunel's project boundaries. Membership records carry project-level principals, sharing classifications, benchmark eligibility, taxonomy, evidence, and audit history. Project access never implies portfolio access, and portfolio access never implies source-record access.

Taxonomy mappings, enterprise identity matches, lessons, comparables, and benchmark definitions are reviewable proposals. Weak name similarity never merges entities. Historical lessons explicitly preserve uncertainty and never turn correlation into causation.

Every metric retains input records, citations, calculation version, inclusion/exclusion rules, missing-data treatment, units, project, and date. Benchmarks are deterministic and expose sample size, source-project count, date range, distribution, method, provenance, confidence, and limitations. Small cohorts are suppressed using configurable minimum sample sizes; suppressed views do not return source citations or project identities.

Supplier, contractor, procurement, workflow, schedule, field, commissioning, turnover, risk, and contract records enter enterprise views only as cited canonical metrics. Brunel does not create opaque vendor scores, worker profiles, or automated rankings. Notifications remain in the local-only outbox.

Brunel never recommends awards or termination, ranks people or companies as objectively good or bad, determines negligence, breach, responsibility, entitlement or compliance, makes employment decisions, infers protected characteristics, treats correlation as causation, or guarantees future outcomes.
